Structure of a mechanical keyboard

Understanding the different components to a mechanical keyboard

Fucntions of the different parts

  • Key Switches: Determine typing feel and include options like linear, tactile, clicky and silent switches.
  • Keycaps: Visible and touchable parts that can be customised for both aesthetics and feel.
  • PCB (Printed Circuit Board): Acts as the central nervous system, responsible for communicating with the computer.
  • Shell/Case and Mounting Plate: House the components and can be customised.
  • Backlighting: Enhances visibility and aesthetics; customisable RGB lighting options available.

Switches

Discovering the typing sensation that suits your preference.

Linear


Characteristics: Linear switches provide a smooth and consistent keystroke without any tactile bump or audible click. The keypress is linear from top to bottom, making them popular among gamers for rapid key presses and quick response times.


Typing Experience: Users who prefer a straightforward and uninterrupted keystroke often favour linear switches. They are also relatively quiet compared to tactile and clicky switches.

Tactile


Characteristics: Tactile switches have a noticeable bump in the middle of the keystroke, providing tactile feedback to the user. This feedback helps users know when a key has been actuated without bottoming out the key.


Typing Experience: Tactile switches are popular among writers and programmers who appreciate the feedback, as it can reduce typing fatigue and increase typing speed by preventing accidental key presses.

Clicky


Characteristics: Clicky switches combine tactile feedback with an audible click sound during keypress. The click provides both physical and auditory confirmation of key actuation.


Typing Experience: Clicky switches are often favoured by users who enjoy the audible feedback, and the distinct click can enhance the overall typing experience. However, they may not be suitable for quiet environments.

Silent


Characteristics: Silent switches aim to minimise noise during keypress without sacrificing the tactile or linear characteristics. They often incorporate dampeners or special mechanisms to reduce the sound produced during key actuation.


Typing Experience: Silent switches are ideal for environments where noise is a concern, such as offices or shared spaces. They offer a more discreet typing experience while maintaining the tactile or linear feel depending on the specific switch design.

Assembly

The most exciting part of owning a mechanical keyboard

1. Unpack the keyboard kit


Place the barebone keyboard on a flat surface. The grids with diagonal sockets / crosses are for your mechanical key switches

2. Unpack and secure the switches


Align the diagonal metal pins on the switch with the sockets/crosses on the barebone keyboard.

Gently press down until you hear a clear click, indicating that the switch is securely in place.

3. Decoration


Align the cross located at the back of the keycap to the cross on the switch and press down firmly to secure it in place.
